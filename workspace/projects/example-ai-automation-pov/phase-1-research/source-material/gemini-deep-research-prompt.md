# Deep Research Prompt: AI Automation POV

Research the current landscape for AI automation and managed agents, with emphasis on the middle ground between lightweight scheduled prompts and broad-autonomy agent systems.

The campaign thesis to pressure-test:

Most AI automation today lives at two weak extremes: scheduled prompts that only look like agents, or broad-autonomy systems with too much access and not enough judgement. The more useful enterprise and professional pattern is scoped-agency automation: managed agents with explicit permissions, a narrow tool surface, contextual judgement about when to act or speak, and a lightweight learning loop.

Audience:

- Enterprise AI practitioners and consultants who care about governance, adoption, and operational risk.
- Consultant-builders and knowledge workers experimenting with AI-assisted workflows.
- Marketers and operators who hear "agents" everywhere but need a practical frame for what is actually useful.

Research goals:

1. Map the current public conversation around AI automation, managed agents, agent orchestration, computer-use agents, workflow automation, and tool-connected AI assistants.
2. Identify where the industry is over-indexing on autonomy claims versus scoped, governed, managed automation.
3. Find concrete examples from recent product launches, vendor positioning, analyst commentary, and practitioner writing that show this split.
4. Surface risks that matter to enterprise work: permissions, data access, auditability, human-in-the-loop review, error recovery, cost, security, and change management.
5. Pressure-test the "middle ground" thesis. If the thesis is wrong or incomplete, say so.
6. Recommend 3-5 content angles for a LinkedIn thought-leadership campaign that ladder up to a "consultant living in both worlds" narrative: enterprise AI practitioner by day, builder by night.

Output requirements:

- Use citations with links for factual claims.
- Separate evidence from interpretation.
- Include a "what everyone is missing" section.
- Include a "risks to the POV" section.
- Include a short recommended campaign thesis and three possible hooks.
- Avoid generic hype language. Focus on practical operating reality.
