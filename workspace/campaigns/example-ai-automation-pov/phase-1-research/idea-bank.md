# Campaign Idea Bank

## Candidates

### Scoped-agency automation

Most AI automation is being pulled toward two weak poles: scheduled prompts pretending to be agents, or broad-autonomy systems with too much access and not enough judgement. The stronger POV is the middle ground: managed agents with narrow scope, explicit permissions, a small tool surface, and a lightweight learning loop.

Provenance: recent workspace signals about agent cookbooks, connected-app actions, local/cloud model availability, artifacts, app rules, and agent improvements.

### The tool layer is becoming the real AI product

The model gets the attention, but the workflow layer is where automation becomes useful. The campaign could argue that the next durable advantage is not who has the newest model, but who has the cleanest rules, app context, and handoff paths around it.

Provenance: connected-app onboarding, agent cookbooks, artifacts, app rules, and model/runtime integration signals.

### AI infrastructure for the 5-9 builder is getting serious

The stack for non-traditional builders is moving from novelty demos to real personal infrastructure: local models, connected tools, rule layers, and automation surfaces that can be composed after work. The campaign could frame this as the practical side of AI-assisted development for consultants and knowledge workers.

Provenance: local model integration signal, agent-building messages, and operator positioning around build-in-public and the 5-9 from the 9-5.

## Pick

Selected: Scoped-agency automation.
