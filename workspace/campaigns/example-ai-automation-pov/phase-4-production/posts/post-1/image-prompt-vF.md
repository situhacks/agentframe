Create a single LinkedIn square hero image for a thought-leadership post about AI agents and permission boundaries.

Use a restrained editorial style, not flashy AI art. Warm off-white background, white card surfaces, dark text, muted grey labels, thin divider lines, and one warm accent. Use a geometric sans headline style.

Headline text: "The useful agent knows its boundary."

Visual concept: a simple permission-boundary diagram. Put "Agent" inside a thin rectangular boundary, connected only to three allowed actions: "Read", "Draft", and "Ask". Place "Write" outside the boundary to show that the agent must ask before taking action. Keep the diagram simple and readable.

Footer line: "AI automation is an operating model problem before it is a model problem."

Avoid robot icons, glowing neural networks, cyber gradients, fake dashboards, and generic product-card stacks.
